# From Lab Notebook to Visual Explainer

Most of my notebooks are messy. Arrows everywhere, half-formed hypotheses, numbers circled twice. Useful for me, impossible for everyone else.

To turn notes into visual explainers, I use a simple pipeline.

## Step 1 — Extract the decision question

Before drawing anything, I ask:

**What question should this visual help answer?**

If I cannot answer that in one sentence, I am not ready to design.

## Step 2 — Separate signal from process noise

Not every detail belongs in the final visual. I keep:

- the mechanism,
- the critical variable,
- the output that matters.

Everything else moves to speaker notes or appendix.

## Step 3 — Build hierarchy

I use hierarchy to guide attention:

1. key concept first,
2. causal flow second,
3. optional nuance third.

If viewers need to decode layout before content, the design failed.

## Step 4 — Test with someone outside the project

I ask one colleague not involved in the work:

- "What is this figure saying?"
- "What action does it suggest?"

If their answer differs from mine, I redesign.

---

Visual communication is not decoration. It is scientific method applied to understanding.
